About

Design-to-build oriented Civil Engineer with a background in architectural design, detailing, and 3D visualization. I create visuals that are not only aesthetic but also technically buildable — because I've stood on construction sites and dealt with the gap between design and reality.

I've managed 7 residential construction projects totaling 8,000 m², coordinated subcontractors and permits, and produced photorealistic renderings that clients use to make decisions before a single brick is laid.

Currently integrating AI-powered design tools (Gemini, Runway, Veo 3) into my workflow to accelerate concept development and push visualization quality further.

Challenge

Designing a three-room polyclinic within a single-fronted retail space that is only 6 meters wide. The narrow façade limited natural light, creating the risk of dark and compressed areas deeper in the plan.

Solution

Glass partitions were used to bring daylight deeper into the interior while keeping the layout efficient and realistic to construct. The 3.60 m ceiling height along the main axis enhanced the sense of volume. Custom chrome luminaires were designed and fabricated for the project.

Challenge

The sloped topography and closed rear façade limited natural light and constrained the spatial strategy. The client sought a design that would feel dynamic while maintaining strong spatial continuity and maximum privacy.

Solution

A U-shaped layout was introduced to create a protected inner courtyard as the core of the house. Modern architectural geometry was combined with natural stone surfaces to achieve a strong yet timeless architectural identity.

Challenge

The initial façade design relied heavily on composite cladding, resulting in high material quantities and increased cost. The elevation also lacked depth and architectural identity.

Solution

The façade was redesigned to reduce composite usage and introduce a more layered material approach. Vertical elements and recessed surfaces created rhythm and depth while improving cost efficiency.
